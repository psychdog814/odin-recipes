French Style Mushroom Stew

French style mushroom stew

A french style mushroom stew, featuring a different variaty of mushrooms simmered with onions, wine and carrots. Can be served with egg noodles, polenta or mashed potatos.

Ingredients

  • 16 ounces of chopped button mushrooms
  • 8 ounces of chopped cremini mushrooms
  • 8 ounces of chopped shiitake mushrooms
  • 8 ounces of chopped oyster mushrooms
  • 2 cups of roughly chopped onion
  • 6 tablespoons of extra-virgin olive oil, divided or to taste
  • salt and freshly grounded black pepper to taste
  • 1 large leek, white and light green parts omly, diced
  • 2 medium thinly sliced carrots
  • 3 crushed and minced cloves of gralic
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups dry red wine
  • 1 1/2 cups of vegitable broth
  • 1 tablespoon tamari, or to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper, or to taste
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 bay leaves or more to taste

Directions

  1. combine all four mushrrom types in a large bowl, toss lightly to mix
  2. Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a very large pot over medium-high heat cover the bottom of the pot with one layer of the mushroom-onion mixture
  3. For roughly 3-5 minutes, cook without moving them too much, until they begin to brown and caramelize on one side.
  4. Stir and cook another 3 to 5minutes to brown the other side
  5. Transfer to a large bowl using a slotted spoon
  6. Add 2 tablespoons oil to the pot, and repeat with another batch, continue until all mushrooms and onions are cooked
  7. Season mushrrom-onion mixture with salt and pepper
  8. Reduce heat to medium low and add 1 tablespoon oil to the pot
  9. Add leek and carrots, saute until leeks turn a light golden color and start to soften. Roughly takes minutes
  10. Add garlic and saute until fragrant, about 1 minute
  11. Stir tomato paste, cook for 1 minute
  12. Add flour and cook and stir for 1 more minute
  13. Add wine, vegetable broth, tamari, cayenne, thyme and bay leaves and scrape up the browns bits at the bottom of the pot with a wooden sppon.
  14. Carefully add the mushroom-onion mixture, bring to a simmer
  15. Reduce heat to low and simmer, partly covered, until carrots and onions are tender and sauce has thickened, 30 to 40 minutes.
